The parallax effect is a visual design technique where a websiteâ€™s foreground images move at a different speed or direction than the background.
Scrolling creates a sense of depth and motion, similar to a 3D effect.

While parallax scrolling can make websites feel more immersive and dynamic, excessive movements within parallax effects might affect people with vestibular disorders.
The illusion of movement and depth can cause dizziness or disorientation.

To make parallax websites more accessible, provide an option to disable motion,
use `prefers-reduced-motion`, and ensure all content remains readable without animation.
