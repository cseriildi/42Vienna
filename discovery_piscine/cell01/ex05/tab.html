In the early days of the web, HTML was introduced as a simple way to structure documents. Created by Tim Berners-Lee in 1993, HTML allowed researchers to share information using hyperlinks, a revolutionary idea at the time. Over the years, it evolved from basic tags like <b> and <i> to a robust standard that supports audio, video, and interactive forms, thanks to HTML5. One fun fact about early websites is that many were hand-coded in Notepad, with blinking text and background music considered cutting-edge design. Also, the <marquee> tag, now obsolete, was once wildly popular for making text scroll across the screen.
